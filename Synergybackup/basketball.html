BASKETBALL-2023

Basketball, game played between two teams of five players each on a rectangular court, usually indoors. Each team tries to score by tossing the ball through the opponent’s goal, an elevated horizontal hoop and net called a basket.🏀
MODE : OFFLINE
DATES : 22 August,2023
DEADLINE to submit registrations : August 20, 2023

Registerations Closed



Rules

1.Each game will be to 11 points or 10 minutes long. The team must win by only one point.
2.Teams score by either regular two-point baskets, or by three-point shots taken from outside the three-point circle.
3.After a basket has been scored or a foul called, a player must put the ball in play from a line past the three-point line above the foul key.
4.All held balls will be put into play behind the three-point line with possession given alternately to each team.
5.On defensive rebounds or steals, players must return the ball by dribble or pass across the three-point line before a basket may be attempted. Once across the line, they do not have to pass the ball before shooting.
After a basket, the team scored upon will put the ball in play.
6.Fouls will be called by the court monitor/referee. A foul called in the act of shooting will result in either two or three points being awarded to the team which was fouled, depending on where the player was fouled and a change of possession. A non-shooting foul will result in the award of one point to the offended team and the ball back.
** Every foul committed against the offense will result in free throws.
7.A foul called in the act of shooting will result in either two or three free throws awarded to the team, which was fouled, depending on where the player was fouled. A non-shooting foul will result in one free throw to the offended team. All free-throws will have players lined up in the lane lines and play live one release.
8.The three-second rule will apply to all offensive players.
9.Substitutions may be made after a basket or an out-of-bounds play.
10.Each team is only allowed one 30-second timeout under the last minute of play.
11.If the game ends in a tie after regulation time there will be a one-minute overtime period. Possession will be determined by the home team shooting for ball.
12.Ties at the end of any overtime period will result in successive one-minute periods until the tie has been broken, with possession alternating each overtime period.
